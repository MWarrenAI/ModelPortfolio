Latex


Latex involves the use of different materials with different properties varied strengths and durability. You can involve 3 types of material: Polyurethane resin, Silicone Rubber and Epoxy Casting Resin.


Polyurethane is suitable for strong pigmented plastic models, Silicone rubber useful for more flexible models via deadening to make it more pliable whereas there is an alternative material which we did not explore in depth.

We explored the possibility of pouring Silicone rubber into a mould to fit our face’s needs but we argued that making this rubber this way would had been costly and would have lost detail in the print we would also have the complication of colouring the rubber.


We explored the possibility of using Polyurethane but this would have required a mould and would have not come out as accurately or be able to be manipulated with use of Software.


We explored the possibility of using Epoxy Casting Resin this would have required mould and cutting apart parts of the model – this would have not been satisfactory for the purpose of our model.
